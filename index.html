Myers-Briggs test

Ive taken the Myers-Briggs test a few times over the years, out of curiosity, and I've noticed that I've been quite consistent with my personality type; The Campaigner, or ENFP.

According to the test, ENFPs are “social butterflies” who are friendly, warm, enthusiastic, outgoing, lively, and upbeat. The types of people who love to spend time and make emotional connections with others, and are often the “life of the party” since they tend to stand out. ENFPs are free-spirits who are strongly passionate with the things they're interested in, and are incredibly creative with their imaginations. Unfortunately, they can be quite a bit too disorganized and overthink because they get too attached to people.

With all of this in mind, I'd like to say that I'm not really that extroverted, I would consider myself more of an ambivert – shy with new people but very loud and chaotic with people they're familiar with – but overall I believe that the test is quite accurate. Those who know me say that I've got a pleasant disposition, with a calming yet chaotic vibe, and it's true that I've got a whole lot of ideas and concepts in my head, and indeed tend to hyperfixate on things that fascinates me.

In job terms, I feel like this would make me a wonderful team player, since I'm quite good at cooperating with team members, and I'm incredibly open-minded to diverse types of personalities and strive to understand different people in-depth. I prefer not to be a leader because although I enjoy taking charge and initiative with my ideas, I find it hard to put in the hard work when I'm not 100% into the project, and if it goes south, I'll probably overthink it to the point of a panic attack.

My Project Idea

A Small Overview

I've got a couple of ideas for a project but, one that really stands out to me for this current assignment is a type of Tinder-Discord-like hybrid website, that you could use to make friends from University, a website called “TemanUni”, or Uni Friend

Why did I decide to make this?

Before you think “Oh but discord servers exists for this reason”, I want you to keep in mind, just how time consuming it is to search and privately message a person one-by-one just to make a friend or acquaintance, especially when you're a shy introvert, or you're from overseas and don't even know where to start, it can be very overwhelming for first-time uni students, even more so for those who just graduated from high school and are moving into a completely foreign country alone – just like me! :)

A little dive into what the website would be like, its features, descriptions and so on

This website will offer a similar matchmaking feature to Tinder, where you list your major, name of university, year or semester, subjects taken, country of origin and languages spoken, along with interests and hobbies that'll hopefully be a fun conversation starter. Once you make your match, you'll be able to chat with them and share your social media accounts with each other so you'll be able to chat more comfortably, the website acts more as a place to meet new people and message them temporarily, so its features are a little bit limited, because I don't think it would need much anyway.

To prevent scammers, groomers, predators, and overall bad people coming onto the website, people can only sign up with their university email, a real one, ones provided from their university, with a two-factor authentication

What are the tools, technologies and skills required to make this website?

As it is a website, I'll need a good amount of knowledge in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and other types of programming such as Python, along with a couple of open and closed sourced tools, ones that I have yet to find. Since this website solely relies on the internet and online services, it would need a good cloud service provider such as Amazon web services (AWS). Signing up is required using Microsoft Outlook (or whatever email provider the university uses) and one-time passwords are sent through there for security.

In terms of hardware, it would be preferred to have a powerful PC, one that is reliable and does not overheat easily, and a lot of backup space just in case anything were to happen. Although the plans and in-depth details for this website are yet to be perfected, I am confident that I can learn the skills that I need to use for this project through RMIT's courses throughout the years of my studies.

The outcome

The outcome of this project would be, once again, a place where first time uni students can meet people from their year without the hassle of searching server after server in apps like Discord or Instagram, and I hope that with this website, International first year students like me will find making university friends a whole lot easier. It would take a lot of time and effort in order to make this website work, but I believe that if i try hard enough, I would be able to make it a reality one day.
